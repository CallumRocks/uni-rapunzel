“Cut off that hair! You’re a grown man! Act like one!” her mother screamed. A string of words she heard every time she visited. No one understood how she felt. People whispered her name in conversations “such an embarrassment”. They laughed at her calling her a “He-She” a “Thing”. She was misrepresented by her body; like an uncomfortable piece of clothing she couldn’t remove.

One day her parents had enough. Banishing Rapunzel to a tower to be alone, segregated from society. Everyday she sat by her window staring at the spaceships fly by, she grew more alone and became depressed. Her mother would visit shouting “Rapunzel, Rapunzel, Let down your hair!”.

As days became months and months became years Rapunzel’s hair grew longer and longer and her heart heavier and heavier. Then one day a large robot was wandering the forest and passed by the tower as Rapunzel’s mother visited. The robot listened as the mother again shouted “Rapunzel, Rapunzel, Let down your hair!” The robot was mesmerized by Rapunzel’s long hair.

The following day the robot returned and shouted “Rapunzel, Rapunzel, Let down your hair!” Shocked by the robot’s presence, Rapunzel let down her hair for the company she yearned for. They talked long into the night and by sunrise were deeply in love. They hatched a plan to escape the tower. Fleeing the tower they ran to the nearest port and boarded a ship and set sail for the stars to live happily ever after.

Rapunzel

It was the year 2315. He was born in a time when robots stalked the land and zeppelins roamed the red air. Unwanted and put up for adoption, he was discovered by a couple who desperately wanted a child. Growing up he knew he was different. Different from what his parents wanted him to be. "He" felt uncomfortable. "He" was wrong. He was a she. Refusing her birth name, she called herself Rapunzel.
